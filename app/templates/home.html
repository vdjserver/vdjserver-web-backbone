News & Announcements

5.5.14 - VDJServer Beta is Live!

The VDJ Server project is happy to announce its beta release at the IMMUNOLOGY 2014™ AAI Annual Meeting, May 2-6, in Pittsburgh. Create projects, upload files within projects, and work together with other colleages. Analyze data with a customizable pre-processing pipeline for filtering sequencing data and perform repertoire characterization with igBlast. Visualize your results at each step with interactive charts. Your data is securely stored on a powerful and scalable high performance data system, so it is accessible to you and those you share it with whenever you need it. Analyses are run on the Lonestar supercomputer, providing resources that scale with any project. We’re just getting started, so leave us feedback, and keep an eye out for updates. In the coming months we will be adding a lot of features, interface improvements, software, and tutorials.

About:

Funded by a National Institutes of Health (NIH) research grant (#1R01A1097403-01), the “VDJ Server” project is led by The University of Texas Southwestern (UTSW) Medical Center in collaboration with the J. Craig Venter Institute and Yale University. The Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC) at The University of Texas at Austin leads the cyberinfrastructure implementation, including the high performance computing (HPC) systems, storage, and software solutions.
